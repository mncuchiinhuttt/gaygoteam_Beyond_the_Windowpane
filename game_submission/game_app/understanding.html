Beyond the Window Frame

Understanding An's Journey

These reflections capture how the world feels from An's perspective, offering gentle insight into the symptoms that shape his path toward healing.

Anhedonia (Loss of Interest)

An no longer finds joy in the rituals that once defined him. He walks away from football, meals taste like ash, and even the thought of his friends' easy chatter feels exhausting.

Persistent Sadness and Emptiness

He describes himself as hollow, carrying a sense of emptiness that lingers through every ending. In the Unending Grey path, that ache stretches toward a future he cannot yet brighten.

Cognitive Difficulties

The fog rolling in becomes An's world. Focus slips away, his mind turns static-filled blank, and every thought feels water-logged, out of reach and heavy.

Social Withdrawal and Isolation

He retreats to the safety of his room, sealed within The Bubble. Headphones become his shield, and distance from Linh feels safer than facing the risk of being seen.

Fatigue and Sleep Issues

An confesses he barely sleeps as it is, his nights restless and unfinished. Even his teacher notices the tiredness etched across his writing.

Feelings of Worthlessness and Guilt

Criticism from his mother leaves An adrift in self-blame. He is trapped between the weight of being a good son and the fear that he is already failing.

Extreme Academic Pressure

The terror of anything less than perfection tightens around him. Academic expectations become both catalyst and burden, deepening the roots of his depression.
